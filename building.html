Building

Building is a very important skill if not, the most important skill in fortnite and will be the key to outplaying your opponents. In fortnite there are materials: wood,brick,metal. Every building piece takes 10 materials from which every type of material you select. Also there are only 4 different type of "builds",this includes walls, floors, stairs, and pyramids/cones. Every individual piece serves its own purpose however, and I bet you already know the basics, so I'sm going to explain the more advanced techniques into becoming a pro.

In this page, I am going to talk about the most important building techinques, this includes:

  • 90s
  • Coning
  • Jump outs
  • Block Offs

90s

In fortnite a huge building strategy is to take highground, this is basically where you try your best to build above your opposing enemy/enemies, doing so there is a very fast and advanced techinque to overtake highground very easily, and this method is called"90ing". The term "90"is basically stacking up really fast by rotating 90 degrees each time. Learning this technique might take a while but it is definitely worth the time spending. There are many different ways pros like to 90, but I will only explain the Classic 90. The Classic 90 is basically where you put one stair, then a wall directly infront and above the stair, then rotating a 90 degrees (clock or counter ) to place another wall, then another wall below the previous one, a floor and jump, and then finally another stair 90 degrees (counter or clock) from the previous stair. You can repeat the process as many times as you want. Here's a clip to make it easier:

Coning

This method is mostly used when you are on the low-ground and your opponent has high-ground. The term "Coning"is basically when you place a pyramid or sometimes even a floor on the opponent who has the highground. Once you cone off the opponent most of the time you will do a jumpout or if you have traps, you may trap your opponent. Traps are basically items that can be found throughout the game within the ground, chests, player loot, etc, and does 150 damage if the opponent is within range and can only be placed on floors and walls. When you cone your opponent, to trap them, you will need to place a wall surrounding them and then placing the trap. There are ways to prevent this from happening for example: placing a wall and floor so it's not possible to cone, or preplacing the pyramid/floor yourself and editing.
